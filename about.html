The Internet In One Sentence

The Internet is the world's biggest network where billions of computers, smartphones, servers, tablets, smart TVs, gaming consoles, and even smart home devices communicate with each other.

Instead of one giant computer, the Internet is actually millions of smaller networks connected together. That's why it is called the "Network of Networks."

🌍 Understanding The Internet

What Exactly Is The Internet?

The Internet isn't a single computer. It is a massive collection of networks connected together, allowing billions of devices to communicate within seconds.

🌐

Network of Networks

The Internet is made by connecting millions of smaller computer networks around the world.

📦

Data Travels

Every message, photo, email, video and website travels as small packets of data.

⚡

Lightning Fast

Information reaches another country in just a few milliseconds through fiber optic cables and satellites.

🍕 Everyday Example

What Happens When You Search "Best Pizza Near Me"

1️⃣ You type "Best Pizza Near Me" in Google.
2️⃣ Your browser sends the request to the Internet.
3️⃣ DNS finds Google's server.
4️⃣ Google's server searches its database.
5️⃣ Results travel back as data packets.
6️⃣ Your browser shows the results in less than a second.

🌍 Interesting Question

Who Owns The Internet?

🌐

No One Owns The Entire Internet

The Internet does not belong to one person, one country or one company.

It is maintained by Internet Service Providers, governments, technology companies, universities, organizations and millions of network operators working together.
